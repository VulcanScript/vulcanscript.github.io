Hello!

My name is Kenric Freiwald, and welcome to my website.

All my life, I have been fascinated by building and designing things. Some of earliest and fondest memories are conducting design challenges with myself using Knex, Lego, and Lincoln Logs. I remember trying to design a bridge to span a created chasm or raceways to test my vehicles. In high school I gained a love of cars, working on my friend's classic Volkswagen beetle or my 1990 Mazda B2200. I remember falling in love with the design of the Rotary engine learning it in a college class I took in high school, due to it's simplicity.

After high school, I joinded Northern Arizona University (NAU), where I intended to study Forestry. Ultimately, while I still love the outdoors, I decided the field wasn't for me, and had rekindled my love of vehicles and engineering. I switched my major to Mechanical Engineering, and while it was rough for a while, I kept my head down and graduated in 2019 with a Bachelor's of science in Mechanical Engineering and a minor in Mathematics.

My senior capstone project was the Society of Automotive Engineering's Baja design project. At NAU, the project was redesigned/optimized every year. We decided to redesign, and I took up the task of redesigning the drivetrain. I was able to reduce the weight of the drivetrain by 25%, and increase the vehicle's placement in the acceleration event. The teamworking, design, and testing skills are still some of my most valuable.

After graduating, I started looking for careers. After applying for a job I wasn't qualified for (as is a ritual when graduating), I was contacted and politely told that I wasn't fit for that position, but a different position for me might be available at a different company. Eager to work, I accepted the offer to interview.

Within a week of interviewing, I was offered a role of Application Engineer at National Pump Company. Thrilled to be working and eager to learn, I quickly became one of the top performers of the team. My hard work was noticed, and as I had desire to learn the mechanics of the Vertical Turbine Pumps I was selling, I took up a position as a Project Engineer.

Exceling at designed to order, highly-engineered pumps in critical pieces of systems, I earned a Product Development Engineer position, which is the role I'm currently in. As a product development engineer, I work on the exciting new projects National Pump Company is working on, pushing the boundries on our catalogs and capabilities, and challenging myself daily with new and interesting design problems.

I've put together this website as a place to show off my projects, and house some of my calculators and articles I've written.
